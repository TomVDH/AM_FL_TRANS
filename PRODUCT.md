# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Solo: one person (the tool builder) working alone on all translation decisions across 11 episodes of
the Asses & Masses project. Works in long, deep-focus sessions — hundreds of editorial calls per
session. Knows the tool intimately; onboarding is irrelevant. Fluent in both English and Dutch,
expert in the AM character canon.

## Product Purpose

AM Translations Helper manages the full localization workflow for Asses & Masses performance
dialogue: upload episode Excel sheets, review character lines in a game-style conversation view,
apply editorial decisions (register, voice, capitalization, terminology), and export clean
translations. Success means getting through an episode with zero drift from the character canon,
efficiently.

## Brand Personality

Precise, theatrical, uncompromising. The tool is in service of serious editorial craft — it should
feel like a well-weighted instrument, not a consumer app. The AM artistic world flavors it: a
touch of performance-art boldness in the composition and typography, but never at the expense of
the workflow.

Three words: Exacting. Theatrical. Efficient.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS cream: Stripe/Vercel/Liveblocks palette clones — white cards, gray backgrounds,
  blue primary CTAs.
- Excel/enterprise admin: Flat gray rows, dense data grids, 2010-era utility feel.
- Dark for the sake of dark: Black backgrounds as aesthetic posture, not purposeful choice.

## Design Principles

1. **The dialogue is the product.** Translation content on screen is what matters; UI frames and
   serves it, never competes. Every piece of chrome must justify itself against the primary task.

2. **Precision over decoration.** Linear-style: dense, opinionated, every pixel earns its place.
   Complexity is acceptable where it saves time. Ornamentation that doesn't accelerate the
   workflow is removed.

3. **AM-flavored, not AM-costumed.** The theatrical character of the project shows through
   typographic composition, editorial voice, and confident layout — not through mimicry of stage
   aesthetics or pixel fonts as decoration.

4. **Solo expert tool.** Designed for one person who knows it deeply. Power-user density is a
   feature. Discoverability for newcomers is not a concern.

5. **Dark is earned.** The primary experience is dark — long sessions, focused work — but it must
   be a considered dark: ink-toned, typographically legible, not pure-black atmospheric styling.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Relaxed. Solo personal workflow tool. Good contrast and keyboard navigation as baseline, but no
formal WCAG target.
